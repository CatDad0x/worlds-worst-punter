🐱 The Cat Dad Model — How It Works

🎯 The Bet

First Shot on Target — you pick one player to have the first shot on target in a match. It doesn't need to be a goal, just any attempt that forces the goalkeeper to save. This is a prop bet offered by William Hill, Unibet, and others. It's a niche market, which means bookmakers sometimes misprice it — that's our edge.

🧠 The Core Idea — Two Steps

We don't just ask "who shoots the most?" — we ask "who generates the first shot before everyone else?" That's a different problem. We solve it in two steps:

Step 1 · Which team gets the first SOT?
Step 2 · Which player on that team pulls the trigger?

P(player first SOT) = P(team first SOT) × P(player | team shoots first)

Why this matters: Market-implied team strength is used to estimate which side is more likely to generate the game's first attacking sequence. A player with strong individual stats on a passive, defensive team will rank lower than a similar player on a high-tempo team expected to attack early.

⚙️ Step 1 — Which Team Shoots First?

H2H
Match Win Odds + Team Attacking Tempo
We blend two signals: (1) bookmaker match winner odds — the favourite is more likely to attack first, and favourites tend to generate the game's first meaningful attacking sequence more often than underdogs. (2) Team-level SOT rate from national squad data — high-tempo teams (Spain, France, Brazil) generate more early shots regardless of opponent. These are blended 70/30 to get P(team gets first SOT).

⚙️ Step 2 — Which Player on That Team?

Once we know which team is likely to shoot first, we rank players within that team using five factors:

SOT
Bookmaker Markets & National Shooting Stats High importance
The dominant signal is the bookmaker's own "Over 0.5 SOT" market — it already prices in injuries, likely lineups, current form, and matchup. We blend it with each player's shots on target per 90 for their national team (2022–2025). National data beats club stats because roles differ internationally. The bookmaker signal carries ~42% weight; national stats ~18%.
⚡
Early-Game Tendency High importance
This market isn't about who shoots most over 90 minutes — it's about who shoots first. High-press forwards (Mbappe, Son, Saka, Vinicius Jr) attempt shots in the opening 10–15 minutes far more often than ball-retention midfielders. Where available, we use StatsBomb WC event data to measure actual early-shot %. ⚡ badge = confirmed early shooter.
POS
Position & Tactical Role Medium importance
A central striker and a left-back are not comparable. Attackers carry a +28% multiplier; defenders a −28% discount. This is the single biggest within-team differentiator after the bookmaker signal.
ATK
Team Attacking Style Medium importance
Spain's players benefit from Spain's high-tempo attacking system. Saudi Arabia's players are discounted for the opposite reason. We compute each team's aggregate national-team SOT rate and apply it as an 8% modifier — high-press teams get a modest boost, defensive teams a modest discount.
FK
Free Kick Specialist Low importance
Most first SOTs come from open play, but if a foul is won in the first 5 minutes the designated FK taker walks up and shoots. We track known specialists (Son, Griezmann, Bruno Fernandes, Messi) with a +7% boost. FK badge = verified taker.
⚠
Injury / Availability Dynamic
A player with a 10% model probability becomes near-zero if he's doubtful to start. We apply a −65% penalty to confirmed injury doubts. The bookmaker odds often lag on fitness news — this is where we can find real edge. ⚠ DOUBT badge = flagged as uncertain starter.

📐 Reading the Edge

Edge = Cat Dad Model % − Bookmaker Implied %

The bookmaker implied % is calculated by normalising their "Over 0.5 SOT" odds across all players in the match — it tells you what the market thinks. Our model % is our independent estimate. The gap between them is the edge.
